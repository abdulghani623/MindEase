Mental Health Questions

Normal anxiety is a natural response to stress — it is temporary and proportionate to the situation. An anxiety disorder involves persistent, excessive worry or fear that is difficult to control, lasts for months, and significantly interferes with daily life. Read our article on What is Anxiety Disorder?

Common symptoms of depression include persistent sadness, loss of interest in activities once enjoyed, changes in appetite or sleep, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, feelings of worthlessness, and in severe cases, thoughts of self-harm. Read our full article on Symptoms of Depression.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a structured, evidence-based form of psychotherapy that helps people identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors. It is one of the most effective treatments for anxiety, depression, OCD, PTSD and many other conditions. Read our article on CBT Techniques for Anxiety.

Consider seeking professional help if: your mental health symptoms last more than two weeks, they interfere with work, relationships or daily functioning, you use substances to cope, you experience thoughts of self-harm, or you simply feel that you need support. There is no threshold you must reach to deserve help. Read our article on When Should You See a Therapist?

A panic attack typically comes on suddenly, peaks within minutes, and involves intense physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat, chest pain, shortness of breath and dizziness. An anxiety attack usually builds gradually, is tied to a specific stressor, and is less physically intense. Read our full comparison: Panic Attack vs Anxiety Attack.

Crisis & Safety

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call your local emergency number (911 in the US, 999 in the UK, 1122 in Pakistan). For mental health crisis support:

  • 🇺🇸 USA: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
  • 🇬🇧 UK: Samaritans — 116 123
  • 🌍 International: findahelpline.com
