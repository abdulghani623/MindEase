About MindEase

A free, transparent mental health education platform built to make evidence-based information accessible to everyone, in every language.

⚠️Educational resource only. MindEase is not a medical service. All content is for informational purposes and does not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

Our Story

MindEase was founded in 2025 by Abdul Ghani with a clear mission: to break down barriers to mental health information. Too often, people struggling with anxiety, depression, or other mental health challenges cannot access reliable information — either because of language barriers, cost, or stigma.

We built MindEase to be a free, open, and evidence-based educational resource that anyone can access — in 31 languages, at any time, without cost or judgment.

Our Mission

Mental health care should be available around the clock, in every language, without financial barriers. MindEase aims to be the most trustworthy, accessible, and up-to-date mental health educational resource online.

Our Principles

  • Evidence-based: All content references peer-reviewed research and clinical guidelines
  • No fake statistics: We never fabricate user numbers, patient counts or testimonials
  • Transparent disclaimers: Every page clearly states we are an educational resource, not a medical provider
  • EEAT compliant: We follow Google's Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness and Trustworthiness standards
  • Accessible: Built to WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards with 31-language support

What We Cover

  • Anxiety disorders (GAD, panic disorder, social anxiety, phobias)
  • Depression and mood disorders
  • Bipolar disorder
  • PTSD and trauma
  • OCD, ADHD, eating disorders
  • Stress, burnout and sleep disorders
  • Therapy approaches (CBT, DBT, ACT, mindfulness)

What We Are Not

MindEase is not a medical service, therapy platform, or crisis intervention service. We do not provide diagnoses, prescriptions, or individual clinical advice. If you are in crisis, please contact emergency services or a crisis helpline immediately.

🆘 In a crisis? Contact help immediately: Pakistan: Umang 0311-7786264 · USA: 988 · UK: 116 123 · International: findahelpline.com

Our Commitment to YMYL Standards

Mental health content falls under Google's "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) category — topics that can significantly impact someone's health and wellbeing. We take this responsibility seriously. All content is:

  • Written or reviewed against established clinical guidelines
  • Referenced to reputable sources (WHO, APA, NIH, NHS)
  • Updated regularly to reflect current evidence
  • Accompanied by clear medical disclaimers
  • Never designed to replace professional clinical care

For questions, corrections, or content feedback, visit our Contact page. We welcome medical professionals who wish to contribute to or review our content.
